# Day 2: Finding Rest

## Verses

- **Psalm 62:1-2** - "Truly my soul finds rest in God; my salvation comes from him. Truly he is my rock and my salvation; he is my fortress, I will never be shaken."
- **Isaiah 30:15** - "This is what the Sovereign Lord, the Holy One of Israel, says: 'In repentance and rest is your salvation, in quietness and trust is your strength, but you would have none of it.'"
- **Matthew 11:28-30** - "Come to me, all you who are weary and burdened, and I will give you rest. Take my yoke upon you and learn from me, for I am gentle and lowly in heart, and you will find rest for your souls. For my yoke is easy and my burden is light."

## Reflection

Why is rest so hard for us? We live in a culture that celebrates busyness, where "How are you?" is almost always answered with "Busy!" We wear exhaustion like a badge of honor and feel guilty when we're not productive. But what if the most productive thing you could do today is rest?

David found rest not in the absence of problems—his life was full of conflict and chaos—but in the presence of God. When he says his soul finds rest in God, he's describing something deeper than sleep or vacation. He's talking about the kind of rest that comes when you stop trying to control everything and remember that God is your rock and fortress.

Isaiah speaks to a people who thought they could find security through political alliances and military strength. Sound familiar? We often look for rest in the wrong places—thinking if we just work harder, save more money, or fix that one relationship, then we'll finally be at peace. But God says rest comes through trust, not through trying harder.

Jesus' invitation is the most beautiful of all. He doesn't promise to eliminate your responsibilities, but He offers to carry them with you. His yoke is easy not because life becomes simple, but because you're no longer carrying the weight alone. When Jesus says He's gentle and lowly in heart, He's telling you that coming to Him for rest isn't a sign of weakness—it's exactly what He wants.

Rest isn't laziness; it's trust. It's the confident belief that God can handle what you can't. It's choosing to sleep tonight believing that God doesn't sleep, and tomorrow's problems can wait until tomorrow.

## Prayer

Jesus, I'm tired of trying to carry everything on my own. Help me accept Your invitation to rest and trust that You're working even when I'm not.

## Application

Take one five-minute break today to simply breathe and remember that God has everything under control.
